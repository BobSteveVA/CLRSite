Who Is CLR?

Community Life Resources (CLR) is a consulting firm whose principals have more than six decades of experience working with state systems to address the needs of people with disabilities. CLR Associates provide training and technical assistance related to permanent supportive housing, transitioning youth, disability service systems, program management and program finance to a wide range of stakeholders including self-advocates, government agencies, case management organizations, service providers, and families. In addition, CLR provides consultation and program support to government regarding program and fiscal management, system design, and quality management. CLR also provides technical expertise to design, develop and implement information management systems in support of programs.

Bob Mirel, M.S.W, Senior Associate, Community Life Resources

Mr. Mirel has more than 30 years of experience in program management and the provision of technical assistance to human services agencies in the areas of substance use disorder, mental health, and mental retardation. He has been responsible for the development and implementation of corporate entities created to initiate significant systems change. His work has included program design, development and management of client services programs, business processes, information systems, financial systems, quality assurance, improvement and management systems, and the day-to-day operations of community-based and jurisdictional programs. Mr. Mirel's work has included the design, development, and implementation of information management systems, supporting programs in the human services community. His focus has been on the creation of systems to provide support for day-to-day operations, management monitoring, and decision support of complex program models. His work has included the development of jurisdiction-wide information management systems that focus on the identification of client need and case management as well as providing a tool for management to monitor progress toward the achievement of program outcomes. Mr. Mirel's experience in technical assistance has been focused on supporting SAMHSA initiatives and support to local and State Government. Key engagements have included provision of technical assistance to SAMHSA and grantees under the Access to Recovery program, Target Cities initiative, SAPT Block Grant Evaluation, Mental Health Services Block Grant evaluation, CSAT Block Grant Technical Review, Criminal Justice Treatment Networks, CSAT Health Care Reform Program and Rural, Remote, and Culturally Distinct Projects. Mr. Mirel was a member of the founders of a publically funded and managed Behavioral Health Managed care organization and a founding member of the team that created one of the first publically managed Housing Development corporations specifically in support of individuals with severe and persistent Mental Illness.
